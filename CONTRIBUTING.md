# Contributing to the PSF Diversity & Inclusion Workgroup

Thank you for your interest in supporting the Python Software Foundation's Diversity & Inclusion (D&I) Workgroup.
We welcome contributions from Python community members worldwide, whether or not you are an official member of the Workgroup.

This guide explains ways you can contribute to our website and initiatives, how the Workgroup operates, how we recruit new members and where annoucements of positions will be made.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> The Workgroup operates under the [PSF Code of Conduct](https://policies.python.org/python.org/code-of-conduct/), and all contributors and participants are expected to do the same.

## 1. About the D&I Workgroup

The PSF Diversity & Inclusion Workgroup is a volunteer workgroup of the Python Software Foundation (PSF). Its purpose is to further the PSF's mission to ***`"support and facilitate the growth of a diverse and international community of Python programmers"*** and to provide guidance to the PSF Board of Directors.
We do this by advising the PSF Board and community on policy, programs, and funding related too diversity and inclusion.

- ### Our Core Values

  **<samp>I. Inclusion</samp>:**<br>
  _We actively create space for marginalized or overlooked voices._
  
  **<samp>II. Empathy</samp>:**<br>
  _We listen first. We seek to understand experiences different from our own._
  
  **<samp>III. Respect</samp>:**<br>
  _We treat every community member with dignity, regardless of their background or experience level._
  
  **<samp>VI. Integrity</samp>:**<br>
  _We're honest about where we are, where we fall short, and what it takes to improve._
  
  **<samp>V. Equity</samp>:**<br>
  _We recognize that equal treatment isn't always fair treatment—some communities need more support to participate fully._
  
  **<samp>VI. Belonging</samp>:**<br>
  _Our goal isn't just to invite people in, but to make sure they want to stay._

- ### Regional Representation

  The Workgroup aims for global geographic representation, with members drawn from as many regions as possible. Our goal is to include representatives from each continent (excluding Antarctica), with multiple members where feasible in regions with large or very active Python communities.
